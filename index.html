01 — About

From the 21cm signal
to galaxy spectra.

I'm an astrophysicist working at the intersection of cosmology and machine learning. I hold an M.Sc. in Physics from McGill University, where I modeled the global 21cm signal to search for hints of non-standard physics in the early universe. I'm now a Ph.D. candidate in Astronomy at UC Riverside, where I build deep learning models that sharpen low-resolution galaxy spectra into something instruments alone couldn't capture.

Before any of that, I was an undergraduate at Sharif University of Technology in Tehran, classifying variable stars with machine learning and falling in love with the idea that data, patiently modeled, can tell you the shape of things too far away to touch.

Outside of research, I'm usually driving up the coast somewhere with an Instax camera, learning to see the world a frame at a time instead of a pixel at a time.

02 — Research

Selected work

Deep Learning · Spectroscopy Dec 2024 — Present

Super-resolving low-resolution galaxy spectra

A generative model that enhances the spectral resolution of JADES PRISM data by a factor of 10, trained with a physics-informed loss function tuned to emission-line accuracy and flux ratios like Hα/Hβ. The approach points toward higher-fidelity spectroscopy for future missions, including the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope, and is being extended into a multi-modal framework that fuses photometric and spectroscopic data.

Advised by Bahram Mobasher & Shoubaneh Hemmati — UC Riverside

Survey Science Dec 2024 — Present

H2O mission catalog

Inspecting 2D spectral data from the Keck Observatory to extract spectroscopically confirmed sources, contributing redshifts and emission-line measurements to the H2O catalog.

Advised by Bahram Mobasher — UC Riverside

21cm Cosmology · M.Sc. Thesis Sept 2021 — Dec 2023

Non-standard physics in the global 21cm signal

Built an MCMC and Levenberg–Marquardt pipeline to estimate astrophysical parameters of the global 21cm signal, using ARES simulations and validating against data from EDGES — in search of departures from standard physics in the early universe.

Advised by Jonathan Sievers & Oscar Hernandez — McGill University

Machine Learning · Undergraduate Jan 2020 — Sept 2020

Classifying variable stars with machine learning

Applied KNN, random forest, and CNN models to classify variable stars in the OGLE dataset, working through the challenges of significant class imbalance.

Advised by Sadegh Raeisi & Sohrab Rahvar — Sharif University of Technology
